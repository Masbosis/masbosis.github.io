Jabberwocky

Digital collage 2015

This piece was created for SIGGRAPH's Artist's Books - Digital Interventions online exihibition and was subsequently feaatured in the showreel at the 2015 SIGGRAPH Conference at the LA Conference Centre, Los Angeles CA, USA.

http://altered-books.siggraph.org/wp/

The piece is a set of six images inspired by the Jabberwocky, a nonsense poem in Lewis Carroll's Through the Looking Glass and what Alice Found There (1871) and also influenced by John Tenniel, the illustrator of that book. The six sections respond to the six verses of the poem (there are actually seven but the last verse is a repeat of the first). Reverse lettering is used to lierally 'reflect' the way Alice discovered the poem in a book, then held it to a mirror to read it.

slinky book

train tickets bound in coptic style 2016

This book is a document of most of my railway journeys between 2005 and 2016, whilst I was employed at Blackburn College. It also contains journeys to and from UK places as diverse as Truro, Haverford West, Newcastle-Upon-Tyne and Dunblane.
